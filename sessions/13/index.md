## 🚧 Fix your homepages 🚧

Check the [students page]({{ site.baseurl }}/students) to make sure that your homepage is linked correctly. If it's not, fix it! This means that you do not have your homepage `index.html` in the root of your repository. You need to move it there. Don't have it in a folder called `homepage` or `home` or anything else.

If I check:

`https://your-user-name.github.io/harmonic-collection/`

and I don't see your homepage, it's wrong.

_Why should we care about this?_

When you make a website, the URL needs to be nice and neat. It helps people find your work and shows that you pay attention to detail.

## Final revision requirements

Let's go over what the final will entail, in two weeks.

From the project description:

### Minimum Requirements

- The website and all the entries must be responsive (work on a variety of screen sizes).
- While each entry will be unique, there should be unifying visual components between them
- All hyperlinks must be functional
- You will make use of a combination of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to communicate a story

On the day of the final you will have 10 minutes to present your site and receive feedback. You will present your theme, and show how you explored it in different ways through your three best entries. You will also show the homepage. Here's a template for how the talk can go:

- Show the homepage, talk about your theme idea / background
- Show an entry, talk about how it relates to the theme
- Show another entry, talk about how it differs from the first entry but still fits the theme
- Show a third entry, same as above

I would recommend running through this a few times, and making sure that your presentation is about 4–6 minutes long, so we can have time for feedback.

I'm not going to grade you on presentation skills. It should be fun, and you should be proud of your work! I'm going to grade you on the content of your site, and how well you communicated your theme.
